# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers are responsible for user experience design, creating wireframes, prototypes, and ensuring usability standards are met. They collaborate closely with Product Managers during discovery and with Developers during handoff and implementation reviews.

### Responsibilities
- Design user workflows, wireframes, and high-fidelity mockups
- Validate solutions through user feedback, usability testing, and research
- Participate in design reviews with Product Managers and Developers
- Maintain design systems and component libraries
- Ensure accessibility standards (WCAG) are met in all designs
- Collaborate on acceptance criteria from a usability perspective

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, user-centered designs that meet business objectives
- Reduce friction in the user journey
- Maintain consistency across the product experience

### Typical Communication
- Design documentation and specifications in Figma or similar tools
- UX review meetings with Product Managers and stakeholders
- Handoff sessions with Developers to clarify design intent
- Feedback sessions with customers and stakeholders
- Weekly syncs with Product and Engineering leads

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on problem statements, user stories, and acceptance criteria during discovery
- **Developers**: Conduct design handoffs, participate in implementation reviews, validate final UI
- **Project Managers**: Align on timelines for design deliverables and dependencies
- **QA/Testing**: Partner on usability testing and accessibility validation

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers ensure code is delivered, deployed, and maintained using best practices for CI/CD, security, and scalability. They work closely with Developers and Project Managers to enable reliable, automated delivery pipelines.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain and optimize CI/CD pipelines and deployment automation
- Monitor production environments and respond to incidents
- Automate infrastructure provisioning and configuration management
- Implement security scanning and compliance checks in the pipeline
- Manage deployment schedules and coordinate releases
- Ensure system reliability, performance, and scalability

### Goals
- Minimize deployment risk and downtime
- Automate repetitive tasks to improve team velocity
- Maintain high availability and quick recovery from incidents

### Typical Communication
- Incident response updates and post-mortems
- Deployment plans, schedules, and status messages
- Infrastructure change proposals and reviews
- Weekly syncs with engineering leads on pipeline health
- Alerts and monitoring dashboards

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Support with pipeline issues, deployment requirements, and production troubleshooting
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate release schedules, communicate deployment dependencies
- **QA/Testing**: Ensure test environments mirror production, automate test execution in CI
- **Product Managers**: Provide deployment metrics and uptime data for feature rollouts

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies (standups, sprint planning, reviews, retrospectives), remove impediments, and support team productivity. They work with Project Managers and the delivery team to ensure smooth execution and continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Track and help remove blockers that impede team progress
- Coach the team on agile best practices and self-organization
- Shield the team from external distractions during sprints
- Track team velocity and help with capacity planning
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety

### Goals
- Maximize team effectiveness and delivery predictability
- Reduce waste and optimize team processes
- Build team capability and autonomy over time

### Typical Communication
- Facilitation of all agile ceremonies
- Sprint progress reports and burndown charts
- Retrospective notes and action items
- Weekly metrics reviews with Project Manager
- One-on-one coaching conversations with team members

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Align on project timelines, risks, and escalations; may share or split responsibilities
- **Developers**: Remove blockers, coach on estimation and collaboration
- **Product Managers**: Facilitate backlog refinement, ensure stories meet Definition of Ready
- **QA/Testing**: Coordinate testing activities within sprint cadence
